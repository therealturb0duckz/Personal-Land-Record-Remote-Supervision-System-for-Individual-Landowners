## ðŸ”¥ Pain Points Addressed

### **1. Lack of supervision for inherited land**
- Heirs living in another city/country cannot monitor their land.  
- No centralized record of who is responsible for checking the land.  
- The system allows assigning supervisors and tracking their activities.

### **2. Loss of agricultural knowledge across generations**
- Elders often hold key knowledge (soil type, crop cycles) in memory.  
- Future generations cannot continue operations because nothing is recorded.  
- The database provides a long-term, structured record of everything about the land.

### **3. Paper-based or memory-based record-keeping**
- Prone to loss, inconsistency, or missing data.  
- Difficult to update or share among family members.  
- The database replaces this with reliable, searchable digital records.

### **4. Poor decision-making due to missing data**
- Without tracking fertilizer use, crop types, expenses, or maintenance,  
  landowners cannot plan effectively.
- The system supports better decisions through organized data storage.
